1967

Tessy Thomas studied in St. Michael's Higher Secondary School and St. Joseph's Girl's Higher Secondary School in Alappuzha.

1980

She also has an M.Tech in Guided Missile from the Institute of Armament Technology, Pune (now known as the Defence Institute of Advanced Technology).[10] She also pursued MBA in Operations Management and Ph.D. in guidance missile under the Defence Research and Development Organisation

1988

July 2002

she worked on the design and developments of the new generation ballistic missile, Agni. She was appointed by A. P. J. Abdul Kalam for the Agni Project. In addition, Thomas was the associate project director of the 3,000 km range Agni-III missile project.

2004

She was the project director for mission Agni IV which was successfully tested in 2011.

2009

Thomas was appointed as the project director of the 5,000 km range Agni-V, which was successfully tested on 19 April 2012

2018

she became the Director-General, Aeronautical Systems of DRDO.

October 2018

Tessy Thomas received the Lal Bahadur Shastri National Award for her contribution for making India self-reliant in the field of missile technology

2018-

She is a fellow at the Indian National Academy of Engineering (INAE), Institution of Engineers-India (IEI) and Tata Administrative Service (TAS)

Dr. Tessy Thomas

Tessy Thomas (born April 1963) is an Indian scientist and Director General of Aeronautical Systems and the former Project Director for Agni-IV missile in Defence Research and Development Organisation.She is the first ever woman scientist to head a missile project in India.
